## SECTION 5: FAQ

**Headline:** Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need to own an instrument before starting?**
Yes, owning or renting an instrument is necessary for regular practice at home. Your instructor can provide guidance on selecting an appropriate instrument based on your needs and budget.

**Can my child learn multiple instruments at once?**
Yes, many students choose to learn multiple instruments simultaneously or sequentially. We offer a 10% discount for siblings or students taking multiple lessons.

**What age is appropriate to start lessons?**
Children can typically begin violin, piano, or ukulele around age 4–5. Guitar and cello lessons are generally more suited for ages 6 and up. Brass and woodwind students are most successful starting at age 8 or 9.

**How often should we schedule lessons?**
One lesson per week is strongly recommended for consistent progress. This frequency allows students enough time to practice and apply what they've learned while maintaining momentum.

**How long until my child sees progress?**
Progress varies by student based on natural ability, practice habits, and attendance consistency. With regular practice and weekly lessons, most students begin noticing meaningful improvement within a few months.

**Can we learn different styles of music?**
Absolutely. Our instructors teach a wide range of styles — classical, pop, jazz, Broadway, folk, and more. Students are encouraged to explore genres that inspire them.

**What is the cancellation policy?**
To cancel or reschedule, contact the studio or your teacher with at least 24 hours notice. Missed lessons without 24-hour notice will be forfeited with no tuition credit. If proper notice is given, we will try to arrange a make-up lesson, though availability cannot be guaranteed. Tuition is not adjusted for missed lessons.
